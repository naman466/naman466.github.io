About This Blog

Welcome

Welcome to my blog on mathematics and machine learning! This space is dedicated to exploring the deep mathematical foundations that power modern machine learning systems. Whether you're a researcher, practitioner, or simply passionate about theory, you'll find a mix of formal rigor and intuitive explanation here.

What You'll Find Here

This blog focuses on mathematically-rich topics in machine learning, including but not limited to:

  • Linear Algebra for ML – Vector spaces, eigenvalues, singular value decomposition
  • Probability & Statistics – Bayesian inference, information theory, entropy and divergence
  • Optimization Theory – Gradient methods, convexity, duality, and constrained optimization
  • Neural Network Theory – Backpropagation, universal approximation, expressivity bounds
  • Learning Theory – VC dimension, PAC learning, generalization and overfitting
  • Advanced Topics – Transformer math, attention mechanisms, generative models, and causality
  • Research Notes – In-progress ideas, mathematical proofs, and technical breakdowns

About Me

I’m a machine learning enthusiast and mathematical thinker with a strong interest in the theoretical backbone of AI. My goal is to bridge the gap between rigorous mathematical theory and modern ML practice—exploring everything from deep learning fundamentals to state-of-the-art models through a mathematical lens.

Research Interests: Deep learning theory, optimization, representation learning, information theory in ML, and mathematical structures behind AI algorithms.

Blog Philosophy

I believe that true mastery of ML comes from understanding its mathematical foundations. This blog aims to:

  • Explain ML algorithms with mathematical transparency
  • Break down complex ideas into first principles
  • Share complete derivations and proofs where possible
  • Connect theory to modern deep learning architectures
  • Foster curiosity, rigor, and intuition in equal measure

Technical Notes

This blog is written in clean HTML and CSS and hosted on GitHub Pages to keep the focus on content. All equations are written using MathJax or LaTeX-style markdown, where supported.

While I assume familiarity with undergraduate-level mathematics, many posts aim to be self-contained, allowing readers to build up from fundamental concepts to advanced applications.

Feedback and Collaboration

If you’re interested in collaborating, suggesting topics, or pointing out errors, I welcome your messages! You can reach me through the contact page. I also encourage academic and research discussion around the posts.
